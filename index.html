Transcription – De laudibus deorum vel hominum (Carmen 1,12)

Folio 7r Page 1 of 2

DE LAUDIBVS DEORVM VEL HOMINVM

Quem virũ aut heroa. lyra vel acri

Tibia sumis celebrare Clyo:

Quem deũ. cuius retinet iocosa nomen imago

Aut in umbrosis Heliconis oris.

Aut super Pindo. gelidove in Hæmo.

Unde vocalem temere insecutae Orphea silvae.

Arte materna rapidos morantem

Fluminum lapsus celerisque ventos

Blandũ et auritas fidibus canoris ducere quercus.

Quid prius dicām solitis parentum

Laudibus, qui res hominum ac deorum.

Qui mare et terras variasque temperat horis

Unde nil maius generatur ipso.

Nec viget quicquam simile aut secundum.

Proximos illi tamen occupavit Pallas honores.

Proeliis audax, neque te silebo

Liber et saevis inimica virgo

Beluis, nec te metuende certa Phoebe sagitta.

Dicam et Alciden, pueros que Ledæ.

Hunc equis, illũ superare pugnis

Glosses

Clio

explanation

una ex viiii Musis — one of the nine Muses; patron of lyric poetry

sumis

addition

contendis — you strive, undertake

Heliconis oris

explanation

in Parnaso monte Musis consecrato — on Mount Parnassus, consecrated to the Muses

aut super Pindo

explanation

mons Thracie in quo Orpheus cecinit — mountain of Thrace where Orpheus sang

gelidove in Hæmo

explanation

mons Thessalie Hemo — the Thessalian mountain Hemus (the Balkans)

Orphea silvæ

explanation

Vocales omnes summe voces dicuntur — all vocal beings follow Orpheus; the highest voices are meant

Arte materna

explanation

musica scilicet, nam filius Calliopes dicitur — music, for Orpheus is called the son of Calliope, Muse of epic poetry

lapsus

synonym

cursus — flowing, running (of rivers)

Blandũ

addition

pro, de — sweetly, with charm

auritas

explanation

sensum audiendi habentes — having the sense of hearing; trees enchanted to listen

ducere

addition

oblectare — to delight, to charm, to lead along

temperat

addition

distinguit, dimetitur — apportions and regulates; Jupiter orders the seasons and nature

Unde nil maius generatur ipso

addition

quasi dicens Iovem super omnia — as if saying Jupiter is above all; Horace implicitly equates Augustus with Jupiter

simile

addition

illi — similar to him [Jupiter]; nothing equals or comes second to Jupiter

Pallas

explanation

sapientia; proxima Iovis honori Minerva — wisdom; Minerva nearest to the honour of Jupiter, i.e. virtue and wisdom are closest to divinity

Liber

synonym

Bacche — Bacchus, god of wine and festivity

saevis inimica virgo

explanation

Dianam dicit studiosa venandi — Diana, enemy of wild beasts, fond of hunting

certa

synonym

inevitabili — with unerring, inevitable arrow

Phoebe

explanation

Apollinem dicit — refers to Apollo, god of light, music and prophecy; Phoebus = “bright”

Alciden

explanation

Herculem — Hercules, grandson of Alcaeus; supreme hero celebrated for his twelve labours

pueros que Ledæ

explanation

Castorem Pollucem — Castor and Pollux, twin sons of Leda; Castor famed for horses, Pollux for boxing
